Texas Hold'em Rules

Multi-stream (shared lobby) poker

Hold'em is only available in multi-stream lobbies (codes starting with lobby-). Single-channel sessions stay on Blackjack.

The pack

Standard 52-card deck (no wilds unless house rules enable them).

Hand rankings

  • Five of a kind (wilds only)
  • Straight flush (royal flush is top)
  • Four of a kind
  • Full house
  • Flush
  • Straight
  • Three of a kind
  • Two pair
  • One pair
  • High card

Object

Best five-card hand wins the pot; all remaining players must match bets to stay in.

Deal

  • Each player gets two private hole cards.
  • Five community cards are dealt face up: flop (3), turn (1), river (1).
  • Use any combo of the 7 cards (0, 1, or 2 hole cards) to make your best 5-card hand.

Blinds and button

  • Dealer button rotates each hand.
  • Small blind (SB) posted by player left of button; big blind (BB) by next player (typically 2x SB).
  • Limit: SB is usually half BB; Pot/No Limit: game named by blind sizes (e.g., 1/2).
  • Optional antes may be posted by all players (house rules).

Betting rounds

  • Pre-flop: action starts left of BB; options fold/call/raise.
  • Flop: three community cards; action starts left of button; check/bet/raise/fold/call.
  • Turn: fourth community card; new betting round.
  • River: fifth community card; final betting round.
  • Betting continues until all active players have matched bets or folded.

Betting options

Fold, check (if no bet before you), bet, call, raise. Options depend on prior action.

Showdown

  • If multiple players remain, last aggressor shows first; otherwise first left of button shows.
  • Best 5-card hand wins; identical hands split. Suits have no rank.
  • Button moves, blinds/antes post, next hand begins.
