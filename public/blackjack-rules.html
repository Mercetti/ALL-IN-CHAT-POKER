Blackjack Rules

Default single-channel game

Core

Standard 52-card pack; casinos often use 6-deck shoes (312 cards) with a cut card to trigger reshuffle.

Object

Beat the dealer by getting as close to 21 as possible without busting.

Card values

  • Ace = 1 or 11 (player chooses best total).
  • Face cards = 10; others = pip value.

Betting

  • Bets placed before deal; table min/max apply.
  • Actions: stand, hit, split (pairs), double (9/10/11), insurance (when dealer shows A).

Deal

  • Players get two cards face up; dealer gets one up, one down.
  • Natural blackjack (A + 10): pays 3:2 if dealer doesn’t also have blackjack; push if both have it.

Player play

  • Stand or hit until satisfied or bust.
  • Soft hands (Ace + other): Ace can flex between 1 and 11.

Dealer play

  • Reveals hole card; must hit to 16, stand on 17+ (Ace counts 11 if it makes 17+ without busting).

Options

  • Splitting pairs: same-rank cards split into two hands; equal bet on each. Aces usually draw one card each; 10 on split Ace pays even money.
  • Doubling down: typically allowed on 9/10/11; take one card only.
  • Insurance: side bet up to half when dealer shows Ace; pays 2:1 if dealer has blackjack (generally negative EV for players).

Settlement

  • Player busts: loses immediately, even if dealer later busts.
  • Dealer busts: pays all standing players.
  • Higher total wins; equal totals push.

Reshuffling

Cut card near bottom signals reshuffle after current round; cards then shuffled, cut, and placed back in shoe.

Basic strategy highlights

  • Hit to 17+ against dealer 7–A; stand 12+ against dealer 4–6.
  • Soft hands: hit to at least soft 18.
  • Double: always on 11; on 10 unless dealer 10/A; on 9 vs dealer 2–6.
  • Split: always A,A and 8,8. Do not split 5s or 10s; avoid 4s/6s vs strong dealer upcards.

House variations may adjust splits/doubles/insurance and dealer soft-17 rules.
